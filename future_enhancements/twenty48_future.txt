To keep users engaged and coming back to your 2048 game, you should focus on features that provide progression, variety, and competition. Here are three key features:

1. Daily Challenges & Seasonal Missions
=======================================
Instead of just "reach 2048," give users specific goals that change every 24 hours.
•
The Hook: Creates a "Daily Ritual." Users will log in just to keep their streak alive.
•
Examples: "Merge three 512 tiles today," "Reach 1024 in under 200 moves," or "Play on a 3x3 grid for 5 minutes."
•
Reward: Completing challenges could earn "Gems" or "Coins" used to unlock your premium color themes or buy more "Swap" uses.


2. Advanced Game Modes (Blitz & Zen)
====================================
The standard 4x4 grid can eventually feel repetitive. Adding variety changes the player's strategy.
•
The Hook: Appeals to different player temperaments (adrenaline vs. relaxation).
•
Blitz Mode: A timer is constantly counting down. Every merge adds a few seconds back. If the timer hits zero, it's game over.
•
Obstacle Mode: A "Stone" tile appears that cannot be moved or merged. It occupies space for 10 turns before disappearing, forcing the player to work around it.
•
Grid Variety: Support for 3x3 (Extreme), 5x5 (Relaxing), and 6x6 (Giant).



3. Power-up Shop & In-Game Economy
===================================
Since you’ve already implemented "Swap Tile," you can expand this into a full "Power-up" system.
•
The Hook: Adds a layer of "Clutch" gameplay where a player can save a losing game, making it feel more like an RPG/Strategy game.
•
The Features:
◦
The Bomb: Tap to destroy a low-value tile (like a 2 or 4) that is blocking your path.
◦
The Divider: Tap a tile to split it back into two smaller tiles (e.g., an 8 becomes two 4s).
◦
The Economy: Users earn 1 "Coin" for every 100 points scored. They can then spend these coins in a small shop to buy "Bombs" or "Swaps."
Pro-tip: If you implement these, start with Local Persistence (using shared_preferences) so their high scores, coins, and unlocked themes are saved when they close the app!
